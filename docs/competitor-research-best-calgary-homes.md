# Competitor Research: Best Calgary Homes / Calgaryism

## Source

User provided mobile screenshots from bestcalgaryhomes.com / Calgaryism on June 12, 2026.

The live site was also reviewed for structure. The site exposes a very deep menu including Home Search, Communities, Condos, Sellers, Buyers, About Us, and Contact. Its condo menu includes All Condominiums, quadrant condo searches, Downtown, Downtown West End, Beltline, Eau Claire, East Village, Kensington, Bridgeland, Marda Loop, Mission, University District, and popular building pages such as Alura, Calla, Evolution, Mark on 10th, Sasso, Vetro, Arriva, Chocolate, Five West, Montana, The Guardian, Waterfront, Keynote, Nuera, The Royal, and Westman Village.

## What they do well

1. Deep topical coverage

They have many indexable pages for districts, communities, condo areas, popular buildings, buyer guides, seller guides, and blogs. This gives them broad SEO reach.

2. Mobile menu depth

Their mobile menu is large and organized into Home Search, Communities, Condos, Sellers, Buyers, About Us, and Contact. The user can drill into multiple search categories quickly.

3. Strong blog engine

They publish visual real estate posts and community content, including condos, new communities, neighbourhood guides, and buyer/seller content.

4. Social proof

They integrate social media, Instagram/Facebook-style tiles, and repeated brand exposure.

5. Sticky mobile actions

Their mobile layout has large bottom CTA buttons for call and text, plus a chat widget.

6. Condo SEO architecture

They do not rely on one generic condo page. They have condo pages by city area, named neighbourhood, and named building.

## Weaknesses we can beat

1. Too much menu clutter

The menu is deep but visually crowded. It can feel like a directory, not a guided lead funnel.

2. Generic branding

The website feels like a team/company site. It does not have a sharp personality hook. We can win with Louis's fighter-in-your-corner positioning.

3. Too many competing CTAs

Call, text, chat, blog, social, search, guides, and many menu items compete at once. We can make the first visit cleaner with one clear primary path.

4. Social sections distract from lead capture

Large social feeds and blog lists can push users away from the core action: search, alerts, value report, or book a call.

5. Visuals are polished but not unique enough

A lot of visuals are standard Calgary/real estate imagery. We can build a more distinctive Calgary condo specialist brand with stronger UX, cleaner layout, and sharper CTAs.

## Our strategy to beat them

### 1. Lead with one clear purpose

Every major page must answer:

- Search condos
- Compare buildings
- Get alerts
- Get condo value
- Talk to Louis

No clutter, no unnecessary distraction above the fold.

### 2. Build a stronger condo-specific SEO map

Main hub:

- /calgary-condos/

Area pages:

- /beltline-condos/
- /downtown-calgary-condos/
- /east-village-condos/
- /mission-condos/
- /eau-claire-condos/
- /bridgeland-condos/
- /kensington-hillhurst-condos/
- /inglewood-condos/
- /lower-mount-royal-condos/

Building pages:

- Use competitor building list as inspiration, but build better building pages with search, building facts, buyer notes, parking/pet/fee notes, and CTA.

### 3. Beat them on first impression

The first screen should feel premium and specific:

- Calgary condo specialist
- live IDX immediately visible
- concise hero
- strong contrast
- trust strip
- two lead CTAs maximum

### 4. Beat them on mobile conversion

Mobile needs sticky bottom actions:

- Search
- Alerts
- Call/Text

But avoid covering the IDX too aggressively.

### 5. Beat them with better buyer tools

Add tools competitors usually do not organize well:

- Condo building shortlist request
- New listing alerts by area/building
- Condo fee red flag checklist
- Pet-friendly condo search guide
- Titled parking search guide
- Concrete vs wood-frame condo guide
- Calgary condo buyer checklist

### 6. Build blog/content engine with search intent

Priority blog/guide topics:

- Best Calgary condo buildings for first-time buyers
- Best Calgary condo buildings with titled parking
- Beltline condo buyer guide
- Downtown Calgary condo guide
- East Village condo guide
- Calgary condo fees explained
- Concrete vs wood-frame condos in Calgary
- Calgary condo exposure guide
- Pet-friendly condos in Calgary
- Calgary condo buildings near CTrain
