## Visual-first page direction

This page should feel premium and direct. It is not a generic home value page. It is a Calgary condo-specific seller lead page that explains building-level pricing, current competition, buyer demand, condo fees, views, parking, floor level, and timing.

Use short sections, strong cards, and one clear form. The page should look sharp and make sellers feel they are getting a real strategy report, not an automated estimate.

## Intro copy

Your condo value depends on more than square footage. Building reputation, recent sales, active competition, views, parking, floor level, fees, bylaws, condition, amenities, and buyer demand all affect what your Calgary condo may be worth today.

Request a Calgary Condo Value Report and get a clearer picture before you decide whether to sell, hold, rent, or refinance.

## Lead angle

This page should capture owners who are not ready to list yet but want to understand their condo value. The goal is to start a seller conversation before they talk to another agent or rely on a generic online estimate.

## Trust message

A Calgary condo value report should compare the unit against the building, the neighbourhood, current listings, recent sales, and buyer demand. It should not be a generic automated number.
